Hi, my name is Tyler Crews (he/him), and welcome to my website!
Tyrowo is a moniker I've adopted. A sort of portmanteau, where Tyro means "a beginner, or novice," and OwO is an internet-ism - it's a text-based emoticon expressing piqued interest or curiosity in something.
Together, they express my overarching goal in everything I do: Enjoy whatever you work at as if it were brand new to you, and as if there is always much to learn.
No matter how good you find yourself at something, if it's worthwhile to you then it's worth getting better at it.

From Scratch

Everything you see on these pages are products solely of my own effort and creativity.
I (hopefully not too obviously) refrain from using any website-creation assistance from products like WordPress, because I'm committed to learning how to do everything myself, with the full range of customizability and freedom of expression that the internet has to offer.
I crafted not only the html and code here, but even the font to create a truly personalized experience and expression of self.
I was able to use the web app Calligraphr to adapt my actual handwriting into this typeface. In addition to using it for this site, I actually use it in my word processors and code editors on my personal computer.
So what you're seeing here is what I write, how I write it.

Self Taught

I graduated from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 2015 with a bachelor's degree in biology and a minor in English.
Although I actually didn't attend any computer science classes there, I made many lifelong friends in the computer science department through my involvement in UNC Chapel Hill Esports.
After working for half a decade as a Sleep Technologist, one of my best friends from school encouraged me to take up coding as a hobby.
So to learn fundamentals I started with free online courses at Codecademy.
Then I learned basic methods and furthered my comprehension by working problems on CodeWars, and used CSS Battle to get a feel for basic CSS properties.
A few months ago I moved on to practice problems on Leet Code, where I work to improve my understanding of algorithms in Javascript, and practice writing SQL queries.
I'd also like to sincerely thank all of my friends who have taken the time to help me with problems and answer my questions along the way.

I seriously go through a lot of notebooks.
(that's why my site is notebook themed)
